During my tenure as the Manager of the Surveying and GIS department In KOM Consult, I prepared several bid proposals, and I am happy to inform that we won three bids of which 2 were funded by the funded and one was funded by the Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ). The projects had Surveying, GIS and Remote sensing input

  • Name of project; Consultancy Services for Surveying and Mapping of National AgriculturalResearch Organisation institute Land in 47 districts of Uganda. Excecuted between July 2018 to January 2020 . The Client is Minister of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries. This project involved mapping all land owned by the National Agricultural Research Organisation institute. Major outputs were processing title deeds for the lands, demarcation of the lands, a Land information system and spatial database that shows all attributes of the lands in the different districts.

  • Consultancy services for mapping of land parcels (Kibanja) of bona fide and lawful tenants on private Mailo in selected sub-counties in the Districts of Mityana, Kassanda and Mubende, Uganda Name of project; Improvement of Land Governance in Uganda to increase productivity of small-scale Farmers on Private Mailo-Land (ILGU). Client; Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ). This project mainly included mobile mapping of land parcels and the current occupants of the lands using project specific mobile tablets and software CRISP. Land and tenant information was fed into the CRISP information system in real time as project teams moved from village to village. The end goal was to issue land inventory (map) certificates to parcel holders. Also, tenants were able to know the size of their land which helps them certify their legal occupancy which protects them from eviction. Excecuted between February 2019 to February 2020.

  • Name of project;Consultancy Services to Undertake Cadastral Surveys in Water and Sanitation Development Facility – Central WSDF-C Areas of Operation” The client was Ministry of Water and Environment. The ministry had acquired lands in different parts of the country. The project goals we to map and document the properties of the lands including, the accessibility, size of the lands, points of interest near the lands, natural features in or close to the lands and any other vital information necessary for the development of the lands. Excecuted between July 2019 to May 2021.
