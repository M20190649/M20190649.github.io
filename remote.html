Vegetation Status Mapping in Uganda's Urban Metropolitans.

Vegetation status mapping is one of the most important steps in deriving solutions for sustainable natural resource management and Agriculture. Results from vegetation mapping provide key inventory for agricultural field assessments, classification and archiving of vegetation data (Samrat Deb et al., 2022). With a focus on Uganda urban metropolitans, this analysis focuses on Gulu district which is one of districts that have just achieved city status. Gulu formally became a city in July 2020 and most international urban design specialists, and academic researchers have directed their efforts to examine the most sustainable processes of urban planning in Gulu. This script takes the initiative to exploit remote sensing technologies by implementing industry vegetation indices to derive vegetation health coverage. Results from this analysis provide potentials for environmental monitoring, offering crucial insights and precautionary measures for natural resource management and agricultural sustainability in Gulu district. (The script allows analysis for other study areas by assigning the district name as a variable). The indices utilized in this mapping include;

  1. NDVI, EVI, and kNDVI for assessing vegetation greenness and vigor.
  2. Leaf Area Index (LAI) aids in estimating vegetated area yields.
  3. Canopy Chlorophyll Content Index (CCCI) assesses canopy nitrogen content.
  4. The Normalized Difference Moisture Index (NDMI) evaluates vegetation water/moisture content.
  5. Canopy Fire Potential Index estimates vegetation canopy susceptibility to fire under specific conditions.

Using Remote Sensing to Assess Fire Severity and Vegetation Regeneration

Appropriate assessment of ecosystems and biodiversity affected by fire epidemics is crucial to modelling and forecasting post-fire activities for managing and recovery of affected areas (Chu, T., & Guo, X. 2013). Earth observation measurements have been identified as effective and efficient tools for assessing, monitoring, and predicting wildfires, as well as getting a better understanding of how forest ecosystems respond to them (Efthimiou, N., Psomiadis, E., & Panagos, P. 2019). This study utilized Landsat 8 multi-spectral satellite data to derive spectral indices and employed supervised classification to assess burn severity extent and post-fire vegetation recovery. The differenced Normalised Burn Ratio (dNBR) index derived from the difference between pre-fire NBR and post-fire NBR, Normalized Difference Vegetation Index NDVI and Geographical Information System (GIS) was applied to analyse fire impact for a post-fire occurrence period of nine months. This study highlights results obtained from both supervised classification and spectral indices, showing deficiencies in the use of the current approach while giving an accurate assessment of the results. The Role of Remote Sensing in Flood Mapping

Floods frequently occur around the world, causing a significant impact on life and economies at large. Mitigation and response mechanisms in flood-prone areas are in high demand due to the disastrously impactful and unexpected character floods hold (Sanyal et al., 2003). Attempts have been made in recent years to warn decision-makers to develop mechanisms that provide a good overview for mitigation, planning, response, and recovery for areas at risk. Flood disaster management literature is observable that use earth observation techniques to understand risk and aftermath damages of flood occurrence. Considering the magnitude of impact floods can impose on areas (Domeneghetti et al., 2019), this review discusses applications of remote sensing used in flood observation and delineation, damage assessment and vulnerability. Different stakeholders involved in monitoring, evaluation and management of flood disasters include meteorological authorities, disaster as well as relief organisations. This study provides an in-depth description of satellite, data and classification methodologies applied in flood mapping. It highlights the cartographic products derived from critical studies, as well as accuracy assessments executed to validate results.

Assessing the Impact of Road Establishment on Settlement Using Remote Sensing and GIS.

Construction of essential infrastructure like roads is an important factor causing urban land use changes (Goodchild, Michael F., 2010).. The Kampala Northern Bypass Highway was constructed between 2004 and 2009 and it was opened to traffic on 1st October 2009. This project assesses the impact of road establishment on settlement by conducting a comparative analysis for some areas around the Kampala northern bypass before and after establishment of the road. Using Standard Landsat satellite imagery produced in 2001 and 2010, land-use changes in the last 9 years for areas along the northern bypass were derived. The data was processed and analyzed using remote sensing and GIS techniques. Results show that major changes that have occurred in settlements in the last 8-10 years period have been largely contributed by the establishment of this road. Over all the results point to a decline in swamps, vegetated area, crop land and an increase in infrastructural development (built up area) like commercial buildings, residential building, roads. Evaluate the performance of pan-sharpening techniques to increase the spatial resolution of the satellite images

In this project, we used seven different panchromatic image sharpening methods to find the best pan-sharpening method to improve the spatial and spectral resolution of the Deimos2 optical sensor. The method evaluation consists of two analytical methods, visual analysis and quantitative analysis. Of the seven methods, the nearest-neighbor diffusion (NN diffusion) pan-sharpening method developed in ENVI 5.1 offers the best results over the other methods.

Air Quality Monitoring Using Sentinel-5 Satellite Imagery.

Air pollution in urban areas mainly as a result of industrial and motor vehicle emissions, is currently causing major health problems to people (B.W Bodah et al., 2022). With millions of people facing health issues as a direct result of air toxics, it causes more concern to investigate areas that are at higher risk. This analysis was done to assess the concentration of Nitrogen dioxide (NO2) for Portugal at large. According to the American Lung Association, Nitrogen dioxide is one of the major causes of lung diseases. Results of this analysis show a high concentration of Nitrogen dioxide in Lisbon and Porto which are the main central business districts in Portugal.
